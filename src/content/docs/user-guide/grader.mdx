A **grader** is a teacher who has been assigned to a quiz by a manager. Their job is
to mark the paper answer sheets and upload a filled CSV to Moodle.

---

## Prerequisites

- A manager must have added you as a grader for the quiz (see
  [Manager Workflow → Managing graders](user-guide/manager/#managing-graders)).
- You need access to the quiz (typically the Teacher or Non-editing teacher role in
  the course).

---

## Step 1 — Open the Paper Entry tab

Navigate to the quiz and click the **Paper Entry** tab. If you are assigned as a
grader you will see the **Submit Your Answers** form. You will *not* see the manager
sections (grader list, comparison table, gradebook import button).

---

## Step 2 — Download the grading sheet

Download the grading sheet yourself using the **Download grading sheet** button on the Paper
Entry tab (visible to anyone with the `quiz/paperentry:view` capability, which the
Teacher role has by default).

The grading sheet has one row per student and one column per question, plus any extra
profile columns the manager configured.

---

## Step 3 — Fill in the CSV from the paper sheets

Once students have sat their paper exam and the answer sheets are collected, open the
grading sheet in a spreadsheet application (Excel, LibreOffice Calc, Numbers) and type
each student's answers as recorded on their paper.

### Answer values

Enter the answer exactly as it appears in the question — typically a letter (`a`,
`b`, `c`, `d`) or a short text value (`True`, `False`).

<Aside type="tip">
  If the manager has set up **value substitutions**, you can use the shorthand values
  they defined (e.g. `1`, `2`, `3` or `T`, `F`). The plugin will translate these
  automatically at import time.
</Aside>

### Leaving an answer blank

Leave the cell empty if a student did not answer a question. The import will record
it as "no response" rather than a wrong answer.

---

## Step 4 — Upload your CSV

1. In the **Submit Your Answers** card, click **Choose file** (or drag your CSV onto
   the upload area).
2. Select your filled CSV file.
3. Click **Submit**.

The plugin validates your file immediately. If any answer does not match a valid
option for a question, the import is **aborted** and a detailed error message is
shown:

```
Row 4: "T" does not match any answer option for question "Capital cities".
Valid options are: "Paris", "London", "Berlin", "Madrid".
```

Fix the answer in your CSV and re-upload.

<Aside type="note">
  Your submission replaces any previous submission. You can re-upload as many times
  as needed until the manager triggers the final import.
</Aside>

---

## Step 5 — Check your conflict status and re-upload if needed

After a successful upload, you will see your own **conflict status table** — a
colour-coded view that shows which answers disagree with other graders,
**without revealing what the other graders entered** (each grader works independently).

- **Green cell** — all graders agree on this answer.
- **Red cell** — your answer differs from another grader's answer for this question.

If you see red cells, review the relevant paper sheets, correct the answers in your
CSV, and re-upload. You can re-upload as many times as needed.

<Aside type="note">
  You do not need to coordinate directly with other graders — just fix the answers
  you believe are wrong based on the paper sheets and re-upload. The manager monitors
  the overall status and will follow up if needed.
</Aside>

---

## What happens after all cells are green?

Once every grader's submission agrees on every answer, the manager sees a fully green
comparison table (with student names visible to them) and clicks **Submit to Gradebook**.
Student grades appear in the quiz gradebook automatically — you don't need to do
anything else.

---

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|
| "Submit Your Answers" form not visible | Ask the manager to add you as a grader |
| Upload rejected — bad answer | Check the valid options shown in the error message and correct your CSV |
| Upload rejected — unknown student | The student's identifier in the CSV doesn't match a Moodle account — check the profile column value |
| You can see the manager dashboard | You have the `manage` capability — you are acting as a manager, not a grader |
